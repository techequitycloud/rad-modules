## 2024-05-24 - Over-permissioned GKE node pool service accounts
**Vulnerability:** The GKE node pool service accounts in `modules/Istio_GKE/gke.tf`, `modules/Bank_GKE/gke.tf`, and `modules/MC_Bank_GKE/gke.tf` were granted `roles/storage.objectAdmin` on the GCP project, giving them project-wide destructive access to all Google Cloud Storage buckets.
**Learning:** Default patterns in infrastructure-as-code often copy over-permissive IAM roles for convenience without considering the principle of least privilege. In this architecture, node pools only need to pull container images, which `roles/storage.objectViewer` (and `roles/artifactregistry.reader`) sufficiently provides. Providing `roles/storage.objectAdmin` allows compromised pods to maliciously modify or delete GCS buckets in the project.
**Prevention:** Always verify that service account IAM bindings strictly follow least privilege. Audit the list of roles assigned to custom service accounts and remove any admin-level or broad write permissions unless explicitly required and justified by the workload.
